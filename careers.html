Career Opportunities

  • By David Aarhus

In the todays modern world the cybersecurity industry has become one of the most desirable professions and career choices. The Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that the industry will experience a job growth of over 30% from now to 2029. However with the development of artifical intelligence, it is also predicted for there to be rise in attempted cybersecurity attacks. Company leaders must prepare themselves for the development of cyber warfare. According to Maryland University, a study showed that a computer hack happens about every 39 seconds.

The National Center for Education Statistics shows how the number cybersecurity job postings have grown by a whopping 94% in the last six years, almost doubling itself it less than a decade. Some of the more advanced positions in the industry can make anywhere between $200,000 and $500,000 a year, however most people working in the field admit to working over 8 hours a day which exceeds the normal 40 hour work week that regular jobs usually demand.

Salary Averages

  • Cybersecurity Engineer $99,000
  • Cybersecurity Analyst $96,000
  • Cybersecurity Consultant $91,000
  • Cybersecurity Manager/Administrator $103,000
  • Systems Engineer $99,000
  • Network Engineer/Architect $112,690***
  • Software Developer/Engineer $107,510***
  • Vulnerability Analyst/Penetration Tester $104,000
    • *** These jobs don't require as much education as others. 22% of cybersecurity specialist postings require less than a bachelor's degree.
     

    Though working as a cybersecurity specialist requires select skills and the ability to solve very complex problems, there are positions that do no require a four year bachelors degree to qualify for the Job. For example, in the jobs listed above, A network engineer and a software developer do not necessarily require as much education as other positions.

    Skills Needed

    Linux

    Being able to work with a wide variety of operating systems and devices shows employers your verstility. Linux is desired because of its versatility and broad use.

    Project Management

    IT professionals need to manage many projects at once, and strengthening this soft skill can give you a leg up.

    Information Systems

    Having a knowledge of information systems is essential to a successful career in cybersecurity. An information system is an organized system for the collection, organization and storage of data.
