Avoiding Viruses

Looks scary, doesn't it?

This is what is known as scareware, software which tries to scare you into doing something

Websites like these want you to think you have a virus so that you'll do something you wouldn't normally,
like pay money to a company or install software to "repair" the issue, which will likely install the actual virus.

Watch out for the following, as they are most likely scareware

  • Popups or websites saying your computer has a virus.
  • Messages telling you have a limited amount of time to act.

There are other ways hackers try to steal your data

Phishing

Is when hackers try to get information from you by pretending to be something they aren't. The email above looks like it's from Netflix, but really it's from someone who wants to steal your data, like account name and password.

Trojans

Trojans are programs installed because they look like useful software, but are actually malware. Once a user installs a trojan, they can do things like record that user's data, install more software, or give a hacker control over the device.

How to protect yourself


  • Don't sign into your account through a link in an email. Go to the real website so you know for sure it's the real company and not someone trying to steal your data.

  • Before installing software, search it up online and make sure it's not a trojan. Also watch out for pirated content, as this can contain malicious software without you knowing.

  • Update your software. It is annoying, but as technology ages, people find more ways to hack into it. Newer software, in general, is safer than older software.

Some definitions

  • Viruses can replicate themselves.
  • All viruses are malware, but not all malware is a virus.
